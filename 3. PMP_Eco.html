ECO — Examination Content Outline

Source: Exam Content Outline/ PMI

📌 What is the ECO?

The Examination Content Outline defines the exact tasks a PM is expected to perform. Every exam question maps to one of these 35 tasks across 3 domains. Knowing the sub-tasks cold is essential — scenarios ask which action best aligns with a given task.
👥
Domain 1
People
42%
1.1
Manage Conflict
▼
  • Interpret the source and stage of the conflict
  • Analyze the context for the conflict
  • Evaluate / recommend / reconcile the appropriate conflict resolution solution

⭐ Conflict Resolution — PMI Preferred Order

1. Collaborate/Problem-Solve — Win-Win; addresses root cause; MOST preferred.
2. Compromise/Reconcile — Lose-Lose; both give something.
3. Smooth/Accommodate — Temporarily de-emphasises differences; short-term only.
4. Withdraw/Avoid — Postpones the issue; not a real resolution.
5. Force/Direct — Win-Lose; LEAST preferred by PMI.
1.2
Lead a Team
▼
  • Set a clear vision and mission
  • Support diversity and inclusion (behaviour types, thought process)
  • Value servant leadership — relate tenets to the team
  • Determine an appropriate leadership style (directive, collaborative)
  • Inspire, motivate, and influence via team contract, social contract, reward system
  • Analyze team members and stakeholders' influence
  • Distinguish various options to lead various team members and stakeholders

⭐ Servant Leadership — 10 Tenets

Listening · Empathy · Healing · Awareness · Persuasion · Conceptualisation · Foresight · Stewardship · Commitment to growth · Building community. In agile, the PM is ALWAYS a servant leader.
1.3
Support Team Performance
▼
  • Appraise team member performance against key performance indicators
  • Support and recognise team member growth and development
  • Determine appropriate feedback approach
  • Verify performance improvements

💡 Tuckman's Stages

Forming → Storming → Norming → Performing → Adjourning
Storming = most conflict. Performing = peak productivity. PM adapts leadership style per stage.
1.4
Empower Team Members and Stakeholders
▼
  • Organise around team strengths
  • Support team task accountability
  • Evaluate demonstration of task accountability
  • Determine and bestow level(s) of decision-making authority
1.5
Ensure Team Members/Stakeholders Are Adequately Trained
▼
  • Determine required competencies and elements of training
  • Determine training options based on training needs
  • Allocate resources for training
  • Measure training outcomes
1.6
Build a Team
▼
  • Appraise stakeholder skills
  • Deduce project resource requirements
  • Continuously assess and refresh team skills to meet project needs
  • Maintain team and knowledge transfer
1.7
Address and Remove Impediments, Obstacles, and Blockers
▼
  • Determine critical impediments, obstacles, and blockers for the team
  • Prioritize critical impediments, obstacles, and blockers
  • Use network to implement solutions to remove impediments
  • Re-assess continually to ensure impediments are being addressed

⭐ Agile Link

Scrum Master's primary role = removing impediments. This ECO task maps directly to the agile servant-leader removing blockers in the daily standup.
1.8
Negotiate Project Agreements
▼
  • Analyse the bounds of the negotiations for agreement
  • Assess priorities and determine ultimate objective(s)
  • Verify objective(s) of the project agreement is met
  • Participate in agreement negotiations
  • Determine a negotiation strategy

💡 Negotiation Strategies

Win-Win (Integrative) is most preferred. Win-Lose (Distributive/Positional) is adversarial. Always negotiate in good faith and aim for mutual benefit.
1.9
Collaborate with Stakeholders
▼
  • Evaluate engagement needs for stakeholders
  • Optimise alignment between stakeholder needs, expectations, and project objectives
  • Build trust and influence stakeholders to accomplish project objectives
1.10
Build Shared Understanding
▼
  • Break down situation to identify the root cause of a misunderstanding
  • Survey all necessary parties to reach consensus
  • Support outcome of parties' agreement
  • Investigate potential misunderstandings
1.11
Engage and Support Virtual Teams
▼
  • Examine virtual team member needs (environment, geography, culture, global)
  • Investigate alternatives (communication tools, co-location) for virtual team engagement
  • Implement options for virtual team member engagement
  • Continually evaluate effectiveness of virtual team member engagement
1.12
Define Team Ground Rules
▼
  • Communicate organisational principles with team and external stakeholders
  • Establish an environment that fosters adherence to the ground rules
  • Manage and rectify ground rule violations
1.13
Mentor Relevant Stakeholders
▼
  • Allocate the time to mentoring
  • Recognise and act on mentoring opportunities
1.14
Promote Team Performance Through Emotional Intelligence
▼
  • Assess behaviour through the use of personality indicators
  • Analyse personality indicators and adjust to the emotional needs of key project stakeholders

⭐ Emotional Intelligence (EI) — 4 Components

Self-Awareness — Know your own emotions.
Self-Management — Control your own emotions.
Social Awareness — Understand others' emotions (empathy).
Relationship Management — Influence and manage others' emotions.
EI enables faster, more effective conflict resolution and better stakeholder relationships.

Domain 2
Process
50%
2.1
Execute Project with Urgency Required to Deliver Business Value
▼
  • Assess opportunities to deliver value incrementally
  • Examine the business value throughout the project
  • Support the team to subdivide project tasks to find the minimum viable product (MVP)

⭐ MVP

Minimum Viable Product — smallest deliverable that has real value for the customer. Core agile concept. Always deliver MVP first, then iterate based on feedback.
2.2
Manage Communications
▼
  • Analyse communication needs of all stakeholders
  • Determine communication methods, channels, frequency, and level of detail for all stakeholders
  • Communicate project information and updates effectively
  • Confirm communication is understood and feedback is received
Communication Channels = n(n-1) ÷ 2  |  5 people = 10 ch · 10 people = 45 ch · 15 people = 105 ch
2.3
Assess and Manage Risks
▼
  • Determine risk management options
  • Iteratively assess and prioritise risks

⭐ Risk Responses

Threats (EATMA): Escalate → Avoid → Transfer → Mitigate → Accept
Opportunities (EESEA): Escalate → Exploit → Share → Enhance → Accept
Risk = uncertain future event. Issue = risk that has materialised. Negative risk = Threat. Positive risk = Opportunity.
2.4
Engage Stakeholders
▼
  • Analyse stakeholders (power interest grid, influence, impact)
  • Categorise stakeholders
  • Engage stakeholders by category
  • Develop, execute, and validate a strategy for stakeholder engagement
2.5
Plan and Manage Budget and Resources
▼
  • Estimate budgetary needs based on scope and lessons learned from past projects
  • Anticipate future budget challenges
  • Monitor budget variations and work with governance process to adjust as necessary
  • Plan and manage resources
2.6
Plan and Manage Schedule
▼
  • Estimate project tasks (milestones, dependencies, story points)
  • Utilise benchmarks and historical data
  • Prepare schedule based on methodology
  • Measure ongoing progress based on methodology
  • Modify schedule as needed based on methodology
  • Coordinate with other projects and other operations

⭐ Critical Path

Longest path through the network = minimum project duration. Activities on CP have ZERO float. Crashing = add resources (costs more). Fast-tracking = overlap activities (adds risk).
2.7
Plan and Manage Quality of Products/Deliverables
▼
  • Determine quality standard required for project deliverables
  • Recommend options for improvement based on quality gaps
  • Continually survey project deliverable quality

⚠️ Gold Plating

Adding unrequested features = Gold Plating. NEVER do this — it violates scope even if well-intentioned. All additions must go through formal change control.
2.8
Plan and Manage Scope
▼
  • Determine and prioritise requirements
  • Break down scope (WBS in predictive; Backlog in agile)
  • Monitor and validate scope
2.9
Integrate Project Planning Activities
▼
  • Consolidate the project/phase plans
  • Assess consolidated project plans for dependencies, gaps, and continued business value
  • Analyse the data collected
  • Collect and analyse data to make informed project decisions
  • Determine critical information requirements
2.10
Manage Project Changes
▼
  • Anticipate and embrace the need for change (follow change management practices)
  • Determine strategy to handle change
  • Execute change management strategy according to the methodology
  • Determine a change response to move the project forward

⭐ Integrated Change Control — The Golden Rule

ALL changes to the Project Management Plan require an approved Change Request. Assess impact on: scope, schedule, cost, quality, resources, communications, risk, procurement, and stakeholder engagement. No exceptions.
2.11
Plan and Manage Procurement
▼
  • Define resource requirements and needs
  • Communicate resource requirements
  • Manage suppliers/contracts
  • Plan and manage procurement strategy
  • Develop a delivery solution

💡 Contract Selection Principle

Always select contracts that are mutually beneficial to both buyer and seller. The best contract serves the project objectives — not just one party.
2.12
Manage Project Artifacts
▼
  • Determine the requirements for managing the project artifacts (what, when, where, who)
  • Validate that project information is kept up to date (version control) and accessible to all stakeholders
  • Continually assess the effectiveness of the management of the project artifacts
2.13
Determine Appropriate Project Methodology/Methods and Practices
▼
  • Assess project needs, complexity, and magnitude
  • Recommend project execution strategy (contracting, finance)
  • Recommend a project methodology/approach (predictive, agile, hybrid)
  • Use iterative, incremental practices throughout the project life cycle
Predictive (Waterfall)Best when scope is fully defined & stable, requirements are clear, change is unlikely, regulatory compliance required.
Agile (Scrum/Kanban)Best when requirements evolve, fast delivery needed, customer feedback loops critical, uncertainty is high.
HybridCombines both — predictive planning with agile delivery sprints. Most common for complex, large-scale projects.
Key Decision FactorsLevel of uncertainty · Team experience · Stakeholder involvement · Regulatory requirements · Delivery timeline.
2.14
Establish Project Governance Structure
▼
  • Determine appropriate governance for a project (replicate organisational governance)
  • Define escalation paths and thresholds
2.15
Manage Project Issues
▼
  • Recognise when a risk becomes an issue
  • Attack the issue with the optimal action to achieve project success
  • Collaborate with relevant stakeholders on the approach to resolve issues

⭐ Risk vs Issue

Risk = uncertain event that MAY happen in the future (proactive management).
Issue = a risk that HAS occurred — now a real problem requiring immediate action (reactive management). Logged in the Issue Log.
2.16
Ensure Knowledge Transfer for Project Continuity
▼
  • Discuss project responsibilities within team
  • Outline expectations for working environment
  • Confirm approach for knowledge transfers
2.17
Plan and Manage Project/Phase Closure or Transitions
▼
  • Determine criteria to successfully close the project or phase
  • Validate readiness for transition (to operations team or next phase)
  • Conclude activities to close out project or phase (final lessons learned, retrospective, procurement, financials, resources)

⚠️ Early Termination Rule

Even if a project is terminated early, it MUST be formally closed through the Close Project or Phase process. All bills paid, resources released, lessons learned documented. No shortcuts.

PM Mindset — Traditional & Agile

Source: Mindset_Slides.pdf

📌 Why Mindset Matters

The PMP exam is scenario-based. The "right" answer is always the one reflecting the correct PMI mindset — proactive, ethical, collaborative, value-driven. These principles must become instinctive.
📘
Section 2A
Traditional PM Mindset
T1
Stakeholders, Plans & Change Control
▼
  • ✓Stakeholder identification and analysis is done throughout the project, not just at the beginning.
  • ✓Always follow the plan — no changes without an approved change request.
  • ✓Any stakeholder wanting to change the PM Plan must submit a formal Change Request.
  • ✓All change requests must be reviewed and assessed for impact before approval.
  • ✓Never take actions without first creating a plan — plan before you act.
  • ✓Consult with the project team before making decisions — they have a more practical approach.
T2
Decisions, Tools, Scope Changes & Estimating
▼
  • ✓Final decisions should always benefit project objectives — choose the method delivering the most value.
  • ✓Use inclusive tools — whiteboard with marker over complex software where possible.
  • ✓All scope changes must be assessed for impact on: schedule, cost, quality, resources, communications, risk, procurement, and stakeholder engagement.
  • ✓Use bottom-up estimating, not top-down. More accurate but more effort.
T3
Integration, Lessons Learned & Closure
▼
  • ✓Your main job is to be an integrator of all project components — never focus on one area at the expense of others.
  • ✓Update the Lessons Learned Register throughout the entire project — not just at closure.
  • ✓When closing, ensure all bills are paid and resources are formally released.
  • ✓Projects terminated early still require formal closure through Close Project or Phase process.
T4
WBS, Schedule & Quality
▼
  • ✓The best people to break down work (WBS) is the project team — not the PM alone.
  • ✓The best people to determine activity timing is also the project team.
  • ✓Quality requirements should be defined early and checked frequently throughout the project.
  • ✓Customers are the best people to check deliverables for quality — they are the end users.
T5
Conflicts, Communication & Emotional Intelligence
▼
  • ✓Before resolving conflict, understand the source of the conflict first.
  • ✓Conflicts should be resolved for the benefit of project objectives, not to satisfy one party over another.
  • ✓Before sending communications, analyse stakeholder needs: what, how often, which method, who delivers it.
  • ✓Use emotional intelligence to analyse your own feelings and those of others — enables faster, more effective problem resolution.
T6
Risk, Contracts & Stakeholder Engagement
▼
  • ✓Identify as much risk as early as possible. Document ALL risks AND their responses in the Risk Register.
  • ✓Negative risk = Threat. Positive risk = Opportunity. Document responses to both types.
  • ✓Select contracts that are mutually beneficial to both the buyer and the seller.
  • ✓Engage stakeholders often and regularly — meetings, calls, one-on-ones, presentations.
  • ✓Tailor communications to each stakeholder — confirm understanding and receipt.
🔄
Section 2B
Agile PM Mindset
A1
Servant Leadership & Product Backlog
▼
  • ✓Be a servant leader at all times — empower the team, remove impediments, give them tools, stay out of their way.
  • ✓Engage the Product Owner to document and prioritise features in the product backlog.
  • ✓Only the Product Owner can prioritise the backlog. If PO refuses, TRAIN and COACH them — never prioritise yourself.

⭐ Frequent Exam Trap

If a Product Owner says "all features are equally important and refuses to prioritise" — the correct answer is to COACH/TRAIN the PO on the value of prioritisation. You do NOT prioritise the backlog yourself.
A2
Communication & Physical Environment
▼
  • ✓Use co-location — have the team work in the same physical space whenever possible.
  • ✓Face-to-face communication with a whiteboard is the best and most effective form of communication.
  • ✓Provide agile teams with abundant wall space for writing, kanban boards, and sticky notes.
A3
Information Radiators, Problem Solving & Conflict
▼
  • ✓Radiate information via large visible charts — burndown charts, burnup charts, velocity charts.
  • ✓Project problems should be resolved by the project team. Let the team choose the solution — your role is to coach and support.
  • ✓Create a safe environment for disagreements. Conflict is a positive learning opportunity — never punish for differing opinions.
A4
Kanban, Vision & Motivation
▼
  • ✓Use Kanban boards to limit Work In Progress (WIP). Display on large whiteboard or screen — visible to all.
  • ✓Consistently re-communicate the project vision — keep it alive, visible, and shared across the team.
  • ✓Understand individual team member needs — find what motivates each person and use it.
A5
Clarity, Ethics & Leadership Style
▼
  • ✓Make sure the team understands what failure and success look like on the project — set clear criteria.
  • ✓Be a central figure, not a dictator — guide, facilitate, enable.
  • ✓Have strong ethical values — the foundation of all PM credibility and decision-making.
A6
Retrospectives & Feedback Loops
▼
  • ✓Review how work was completed by conducting a retrospective at the end of each sprint/iteration.
  • ✓Use feedback loops — apply lessons learned from one sprint directly to the next iteration.

💡 Scrum Ceremonies — All Four

Sprint Planning — What work goes into this sprint?
Daily Standup — What did I do? What will I do? Any blockers?
Sprint Review — Demonstrate working product to stakeholders for feedback.
Sprint Retrospective — How can we improve the process and team dynamics?

⚠️ EVM Memory Rule

CV & SV: "EV minus" (CV=EV−AC, SV=EV−PV). CPI & SPI: "EV over" (CPI=EV/AC, SPI=EV/PV). >1 always means GOOD. <1 always means BAD.

⭐ Cost of Quality

Cost of Conformance = Prevention + Appraisal (spending to AVOID defects).
Cost of Non-Conformance = Internal Failure + External Failure (cost when defects OCCUR).
Prevention is always cheaper. "Quality is planned in, not inspected in." Rule of 7 = 7 consecutive points on same side of mean = out of control.

⚠️ Risk Management

Threats — EATMAEscalate → Avoid → Transfer → Mitigate → Accept
Opportunities — EESEAEscalate → Exploit → Share → Enhance → Accept

⭐ Qualitative vs Quantitative Risk Analysis

Qualitative: Probability × Impact Matrix; H/M/L ranking; done first; no special software; ALL projects should do this.
Quantitative: Assigns monetary values; Monte Carlo, Decision Tree, Tornado Charts; requires specialist software; only for HIGH-priority risks.

Contingency Reserve = Known unknowns (identified risks). In cost baseline.
Management Reserve = Unknown unknowns. NOT in cost baseline; requires change request to access.
EMV = Probability (%) × Risk Impact ($)

⭐ Communication Model + Key Terms

Sender → Encode → Message → Channel → Decode → Receiver → Feedback
Noise = anything that disrupts the message.

Select contracts mutually beneficial to both parties.
